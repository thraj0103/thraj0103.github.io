Deep Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Racing (F1TENTH)

I applied deep reinforcement learning to train autonomous racing agents on the F1TENTH platform. The objective was to push high-speed performance while keeping stable control. Agents were trained in simulation and validated on physical hardware.

Methodology

  • Used Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) and Soft Actor–Critic (SAC) algorithms.
  • Designed reward shaping for speed, track adherence, and overtaking.
  • Implemented curriculum learning to gradually increase track complexity.
  • Applied domain randomization for robustness when transferring from sim to real.

Results

  • Agents achieved competitive lap times in Gazebo simulations.
  • Successfully transferred to physical F1TENTH cars with minimal tuning.
  • Stable lane keeping and overtaking maneuvers under dynamic conditions.

Stack

Python · PyTorch · Stable-Baselines3 · ROS 2 · Gazebo · F1TENTH
