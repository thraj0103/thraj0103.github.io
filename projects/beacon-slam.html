Beacon-Based Localization for Multi-Sensor Fusion SLAM

I integrated a beacon-based global correction module into an RTAB-Map SLAM pipeline in ROS 2 to handle GNSS-denied and feature-sparse environments. A particle filter fuses wheel/IMU odometry with distance measurements from fixed beacons to correct drift and stabilize the robot’s global pose.

System Architecture

  • SLAM Backbone: RTAB-Map (RGB-D/LiDAR) for online mapping and loop closure.
  • Beacon Layer: Static beacons broadcast IDs; the robot subscribes to range messages.
  • Fusion: Particle filter combines odometry + beacon ranges for pose correction.
  • Integration: ROS 2 nodes for beacon I/O, fusion, and TF corrections feeding back into SLAM.

Fusion Details

  • Propagate particles with odometry; weight by range residuals to known beacon locations.
  • Resample and publish the corrected pose to the SLAM graph for drift mitigation.
  • Tuned update rates to maintain real-time performance on TurtleBot3 class hardware.

Evaluation

  • Simulation: Gazebo + RViz on TurtleBot3 with configurable beacon layouts.
  • Comparisons: RTAB-Map alone vs. RTAB-Map + beacons; Cartographer (LiDAR-only) as reference.

Results

  • Reduced long-term drift and better trajectory alignment when visual features were sparse or repetitive.
  • Stable global pose under occlusions and partial loop-closure failures.

Stack

ROS 2 · RTAB-Map · Particle Filter · LiDAR/RGB-D · TF · Gazebo · RViz · TurtleBot3
